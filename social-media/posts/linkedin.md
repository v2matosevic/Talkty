We just open sourced Talkty, our local speech to text app for Windows and macOS.

The idea is simple. You press a global hotkey, you speak, and your words land on the clipboard, ready to paste anywhere. The transcription runs entirely on your device with Whisper. No account, no internet, no telemetry. The audio is discarded the moment it becomes text.

Most dictation tools send your microphone to a server you do not control. For a lot of what people say out loud, client work, unfinished ideas, anything sensitive, that is a problem. So we built the opposite.

It started as a tool for developers who code by talking to an AI agent. There is an optional Prompting mode that turns a rambling dictation into a clean, structured prompt for tools like Claude Code or Cursor. But at its core it is a fast, private way to hold a key, talk, and get text in any app.

Both versions are MIT licensed and fully open. Take it, fork it, ship it, or send a pull request.
