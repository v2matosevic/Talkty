## Launch thread

**1/** (attach `video/talkty-promo-horizontal.mp4` for best reach, or `images/hero.png`)
I open sourced Talkty: free speech to text that runs entirely on your machine.

Press a hotkey, talk, and your words are on the clipboard. No account, no cloud, no telemetry. Whisper runs locally.

Windows and macOS. MIT licensed.

**2/**
Why local? Most dictation apps stream your microphone to a server you don't control.

For a lot of what people say out loud, client work, half-formed ideas, anything private, that is a dealbreaker. Talkty transcribes on-device and throws the audio away the second it becomes text.

**3/** (attach `images/pill-states.png`)
It is fast. With a GPU a long clip transcribes in well under a second.

CUDA on Nvidia, Vulkan on AMD and Intel, Metal plus the Neural Engine on Apple Silicon. CPU works too.

**4/** (attach `images/feature-prompting.png`)
It started as a tool for people who code by talking to an AI agent.

Optional Prompting mode turns a rambling dictation into a clean, structured prompt for Claude Code, Cursor, or Codex. It keeps every detail and drops the filler.
